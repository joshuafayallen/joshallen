Introduction

Since may I have had to start collecting data for my dissertation which is exciting and a little scary. I was also hired on to an NSF grant to do some data collection. Based on the data I needed for my dissertation and the data collection for the grant all this entailed a ton of web scraping. Which I had largely avoided before this and felt like out of my skill set. After like six months of having to do this more or less every week I decided to write this post to demystify the process a bit and for me to refer back to from time to time.

My Approach To Webscraping

For the most part webscraping is more like a solving a mystery rather than building a skyscrapper. What I mean is that rather than building a large infrastructure like an R package or an app you are really just figuring out what other people did. I assure you if you stumbled upon this humble blog you have had to do this before! This is no different just either way more fun or way more frustrating(often it is both).

What we will cover

What will you need

In this post I will use these packages:

pacman::p_load(rvest, polite,tidyverse)

These are really the bread and butter of webscraping in R and for the most part the fundamentals of what to look for should translate over to other webscraping tools like beautifulsoup.
